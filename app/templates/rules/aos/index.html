Age of Sigmar — 4th Edition

GHB 2025-26 · April 2026 Battlescroll Active

The fourth edition of Warhammer Age of Sigmar launched in July 2024 with the Skaventide starter set, ushering in a thorough redesign of the game's core rules. Nearly every warscroll was rewritten, army-building was restructured around the Regiment system, and the points economy was recalibrated. The Generals Handbook 2025-26 established the current matched-play framework, and the April 2026 Battlescroll introduced the Cumulative Surcharge rule to balance Auxiliary unit spam.

Core Game Loop

Each game of Age of Sigmar is played over five Battle Rounds. At the start of each round, both players roll a die — the winner of the Priority Roll may choose whether they or their opponent takes the first Player Turn. This creates the risk of a Double Turn: a player who went second last round may go first this round, granting two consecutive turns of movement, shooting, and combat.

Within each Player Turn, actions are resolved in a fixed sequence of phases: Start of Turn, Hero, Movement, Shooting, Charge, Combat, and End of Turn. Both players participate in most phases, either through reactions or through alternating activations in Combat.

Key Design Philosophy. AoS 4ed leans into decision-making over raw dice volume. Command Points are a fixed resource (4 per round), abilities are typed as Passive, Used, or Reaction, and Combat alternates activations so both armies are engaged throughout the turn.

Dice & Rolls

Age of Sigmar uses standard six-sided dice (D6). All rolls are compared against a target number; a result equal to or higher than the target counts as a success. A natural roll of 1 always fails for attack rolls; a natural 6 may trigger critical hit effects depending on the warscroll. Modifiers are applied after rolling: a positive modifier improves the target threshold, a negative modifier worsens it (to a minimum of 2+).

Victory Conditions

Most matched-play battleplans determine the winner through control of Objectives — marked points on the battlefield. At the end of each round (and sometimes during it), objectives are scored based on the Control stat of models within range. The player who scored the most points across the five rounds wins; ties go to the player who scored most in the final round.
