Army Composition

GHB 2025-26 · April 2026 Battlescroll Active

Army composition in AoS 4ed is governed by the Battlepack in use and the Regiment system. Every list is built from a combination of Regiments and Auxiliary units, with the active Battlepack setting the total points limit and the caps on each.

Battlepacks

Two standard Battlepacks exist for matched play:

Vanguard

1,000 points

  • 1–2 Regiments
  • 0–1 Auxiliary units
  • Smaller boards, 4 Battle Rounds

Battlehost

2,000 points

  • 2–4 Regiments
  • 0–2 Auxiliary units (before surcharge)
  • Standard board, 5 Battle Rounds

Regiments

A Regiment is the fundamental building block of an AoS army list. Each Regiment consists of:

  • One Hero leader — any Hero from your faction’s warscroll roster. This Hero leads the Regiment and confers its identity.
  • Up to three additional units — chosen from the Hero’s Companion Units list. Each unit must match at least one of the keyword categories listed as Companions on the Hero’s warscroll. If the Hero has no Companion Units listed, they may not take additional units in their Regiment.

There is no minimum unit count per Regiment beyond the one required Hero leader. A Regiment of a single Hero is entirely legal.

Companion Units are restrictive by design. Not every Hero can lead every unit. A Skaven Clawlord leads Verminus warriors; a Grey Seer may lead Clanrats or Skaven spellcasters. Check each Hero’s warscroll for their specific Companion list.

Auxiliary Units

Auxiliary units are units included in your army that do not belong to any Regiment. They are not led by a Hero and do not need to match any Companion Units list. Auxiliaries are useful for:

  • Including units whose keywords do not match any available Hero’s Companions
  • Adding cheap objective holders or specialist units beyond Regiment slots
  • Accommodating powerful non-Hero Behemoths

The number of Auxiliary slots is limited by the Battlepack: Vanguard allows 0–1, Battlehost allows 0–2 (before the Cumulative Surcharge). Additional Auxiliaries beyond these caps are legal but trigger the surcharge (see below).

Cumulative Surcharge

The Cumulative Surcharge was introduced in the April 2026 Battlescroll to discourage auxiliary-heavy “castle” lists. It works as follows:

For each Auxiliary unit taken beyond the first (i.e. the 2nd, 3rd, 4th, etc.), an extra cumulative points cost is added to that unit’s base price:

Auxiliary Number Additional Cost Example (100pt unit)
1st Auxiliary+0 pts100 pts
2nd Auxiliary+20 pts120 pts
3rd Auxiliary+40 pts140 pts
4th Auxiliary+60 pts160 pts
5th Auxiliary+80 pts180 pts

The surcharge stacks cumulatively: your second Auxiliary costs +20, your third costs +40 (total +60 across both), and so on. This makes stacking many Auxiliaries significantly more expensive.

Command Point Bonus. The player who fields fewer Auxiliary units than their opponent gains +1 Command Point per Battle Round. This creates a genuine trade-off: more Auxiliaries grant flexibility at the cost of efficiency and points, while leaner lists gain an ongoing resource advantage.

Reinforcement

A unit may be taken as a Reinforced unit, doubling its model count and its points cost. Each warscroll notes whether the unit can be reinforced; units that cannot be reinforced (marked “Cannot be Reinforced”) must be taken at their base size.

A unit may only be reinforced once — you cannot take a triple-size unit. Reinforcement applies to the unit as a whole; you pay double the listed points and field double the listed model count. For example, a unit of 10 Clanrats (110 pts) reinforced becomes 20 Clanrats for 220 pts.

Enhancements

Each Regiment leader (Hero) may take one set of free Enhancements:

  • One Heroic Trait — a special rule chosen from the faction’s Heroic Trait table, granting the Hero a unique bonus ability.
  • One Artefact of Power — a magical item chosen from the faction’s Artefact table, adding a weapon or defensive enhancement to the Hero.

These Enhancements are free and do not cost additional points. Heroes beyond the first in a list may also take Enhancements, but they cost Command Points or points depending on the active Battlepack rules. Each Enhancement may only be taken once per army.

The General

One Hero in your army must be designated as the General before the game begins. The General is always the leader of Regiment 1. In addition to their normal warscroll abilities, the General gains access to the faction’s General’s Handbook Command Traits and may use certain abilities reserved for the General specifically. If your General is slain, some battleplans impose an ongoing penalty; check your active Battlepack for specifics.

Only Heroes with the CAN BE GENERAL keyword on their warscroll (or all Heroes if no such restriction is listed) may be designated as General.
