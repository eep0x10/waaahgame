Turn Structure

GHB 2025-26 · April 2026 Battlescroll Active

A standard game of Age of Sigmar lasts five Battle Rounds. Each Battle Round consists of two Player Turns — one for each player — preceded by a Priority Roll that determines who goes first that round.

The Priority Roll

At the start of each Battle Round, both players simultaneously roll a single D6. The player who rolls higher wins the Priority Roll and may choose whether they or their opponent takes the first Player Turn this round. If the dice are tied, the player who did not have priority in the previous round wins automatically; if it is the very first round, the player who finished deploying their army first wins the tie.

The Double Turn

The Priority Roll creates the possibility of a Double Turn: a player who took the second Player Turn in round N may choose to take the first Player Turn in round N+1, effectively getting two consecutive turns. Double Turns are one of the game's signature strategic moments — the opportunity to move and attack twice in a row can be devastating, but surrendering the Priority Roll in the next round is a genuine risk. Experienced players often deliberately pass the priority to bait their opponent into a bad double turn.

Strategic note. Whether to take the Double Turn depends on board state. If your army needs to move into charge range, or if key objectives are contested, the extra turn may be worth it. If your opponent has a shooting-heavy list, giving them the Double Turn may be catastrophic.

Player Turn Phases

Each Player Turn proceeds through the following phases in strict order. The player whose turn it is is called the Active Player; their opponent is the Passive Player. Both players may respond to some events in each phase via Reaction abilities.

Start of Turn

At the start of their turn, the active player resolves any abilities or effects that trigger at this timing. Persistent effects — such as ongoing spells or prayer blessings from previous turns — are also evaluated here. Some warscroll abilities, enhancements, and battleplan effects are also triggered at the start of the turn.

Hero Phase

The Hero Phase is the turn's dedicated window for magical and religious power. During this phase the active player may:

  • Cast Spells — Wizards may attempt to cast spells from their warscroll and any Spell Lores they know. Each spell has a casting value; the Wizard rolls 2D6 and must meet or beat it. A natural double-1 or double-6 triggers a Miscast. The passive player's Wizards may attempt to unbind any successful cast (rolling 2D6 and beating the casting roll). Each Wizard may attempt to cast each known spell once per Hero Phase, and each individual spell can only be successfully cast once per Hero Phase across the entire game.
  • Chant Prayers — Priests may chant prayers from their warscroll or Prayer Lores. Unlike spells, prayers are not unbound; they succeed on a D6 roll meeting or beating the chanting value. Prayers that have already been answered remain in effect and cannot be chanted again while active.
  • Use Heroic Abilities — Heroes may activate a Heroic Ability during this phase. Heroic Abilities are powerful once-per-game (or per-round) effects that require spending Command Points. The active player may use one Heroic Ability per Hero per Hero Phase.
  • Manifest Manifestations — Wizards and Priests may also attempt to summon Manifestations (see the Abilities section for details).

Movement Phase

During the Movement Phase, the active player may move each of their units in one of three ways:

  • Normal Move — The unit moves up to its Move characteristic in inches. A unit that makes a Normal Move may not move within 3" of an enemy unit unless it was already within 3" of that enemy at the start of the phase.
  • Run — The unit rolls a D6 and may move up to its Move value plus the roll result in inches. A unit that Runs cannot shoot or charge later in the same turn.
  • Retreat — A unit within 3" of an enemy may Retreat: it moves up to its Move value in any direction, including through enemy units and models. A unit that Retreats cannot shoot or charge later in the same turn. Retreating does not trigger free strikes unless a specific ability says so.

Units may not end their move overlapping other models. Terrain pieces affect movement according to their terrain rules; most terrain is passable but slows movement or requires a roll.

Shooting Phase

During the Shooting Phase, each eligible unit may shoot with its ranged weapons. A unit may shoot if it did not Run or Retreat this turn and is not within 3" of an enemy unit (unless it has an ability that permits shooting in combat). Each ranged weapon attack follows the attack sequence: roll to Hit, roll to Wound, opponent rolls to Save, apply Damage.

Charge Phase

During the Charge Phase, any eligible unit may attempt to charge an enemy unit within 12". The charging player rolls 2D6 — if the result meets or exceeds the distance to the nearest enemy model in the target unit, the charge succeeds and the unit moves up to the rolled distance, ending within 0.5" of the target. A failed charge means the unit does not move. Units that Ran or Retreated this turn cannot charge.

A unit that successfully charges may use a Charge Reaction ability if it has one. The charged unit (or another nearby unit) may also use a Reaction to this event.

Combat Phase

The Combat Phase uses alternating activations: the active player picks a unit to fight, resolves all its attacks, then the passive player picks a unit to fight, and so on until all eligible units have fought. The active player always chooses first.

To be eligible to fight, a unit must either:

  • Be within 3" of an enemy unit, or
  • Have made a successful charge this turn.

When a unit fights, it first piles in up to 3" toward the nearest enemy model, then each model in the unit attacks with all its melee weapons against enemy units within reach. The attack sequence is the same as for shooting: Hit, Wound, Save, Damage. Once all of a unit's attacks are resolved, control passes back to the opponent to activate a unit.

Pile-in clarification. Pile-in moves must end at least as close to the nearest enemy as before the move. Models may pile in even if they are not in base contact, as long as they end within 3" of an enemy. This allows units to wrap around or consolidate after a losing fight.

End of Turn

At the end of the active player's turn, any effects that last “until the end of your turn” expire. Objectives are checked for control if the battleplan specifies it. Some abilities trigger here. After the End of Turn, the other player begins their Player Turn (following the same phase sequence). Once both players have completed their Player Turn, the Battle Round ends and a new one begins with a fresh Priority Roll.

Command Points

Each player receives 4 Command Points at the start of every Battle Round. Command Points do not carry over between rounds — any unspent points are lost. They are spent to activate Command Abilities, which appear on warscrolls, in rules supplements, and on army enhancements. Common uses include:

  • All-Out Attack — Re-roll Hit rolls of 1 for a unit (1 CP).
  • All-Out Defence — Re-roll Save rolls of 1 for a unit (1 CP).
  • Inspiring Presence — Ignore Battleshock test for a unit (1 CP).
  • Various warscroll-specific Command Abilities.

Because Command Points reset each round, efficient spending within a single round is critical. Players who control fewer Auxiliary units than their opponent gain a bonus Command Point per round (see Army Composition for details).
